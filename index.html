Hypothesis: With climate change, we predict that there is a greater strain on quality of life

Decompose

  1. With climate change, we predict that there is a greater strain on quality of life
  2. Defining climate change as extremes in both heat index, wind speed, and precipitation
  3. Quality-of-life measures are defined by crime rates, property values, and traffic fatalities
  4. Four of the most populous cities in the United States: New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, and Philadelphia
  5. Measuring our hypothesis with U.S. cities allows us to measure climate change on quality of life without having to resolve socioeconomic factors that would interfere with measuring the data set

Data Retrieval

  • Quality-of-life measures began by finding the Open Data Websites for the four major cities
  • Traffic Fatality
  • Federal Housing Finance Agency
  • Weather from Meteostat.net
  • Due to data availability we decided on 2012 and 2019. Earlier than 2012 datasets were not available for all cities.

Data Cleaning

  • Jupyter Lab was used heavily to clean the data
  • Traffic Fatalities, Property Values, and Weather data sets were less cumbersome
  • Removed & renamed columns
  • Datetimeindex
  • Groupby month
  • Valuetype error
